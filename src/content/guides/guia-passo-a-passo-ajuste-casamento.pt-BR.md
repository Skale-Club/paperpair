# Step-by-Step Guide: Marriage Green Card (Adjustment of Status)

> Educational and general content. Forms, fees, and policies change frequently. Always confirm on USCIS.gov before filing.

## Most Important Points

- Critical — Do not travel without approved advance parole: the beneficiary should not leave the U.S. during the process without valid travel authorization. An unapproved trip can abandon the adjustment and cause serious immigration consequences.
- Urgent — Joint sponsor when primary income is insufficient: if the primary sponsor does not meet the I-864P minimum, the joint sponsor must prove their own qualifying income for the applicable household size.
- Advantage — Immediate-relative spouse protections: many U.S. citizen spouse cases have important protections for adjustment of status, including some overstay scenarios after a lawful entry.

### Recommended next steps (4-week roadmap)

1. Week 1: request long-form birth certificate and start certified translations.
2. Week 2: schedule the I-693 medical exam with a USCIS civil surgeon.
3. Week 3: gather complete financial evidence for sponsorship and joint sponsorship.
4. Week 4: prepare the filing strategy (including how to send the I-130 and adjustment packet, per current instructions).

## STEP-BY-STEP GUIDE

Use this list in chronological order and check each item as you finish.

### Step 1: Gather essential personal documents

- [ ] Beneficiary passport: copy of biographic page, visa, and entry stamps.
- [ ] Beneficiary I-94: download from the official CBP portal.
- [ ] Beneficiary birth certificate + certified English translation.
- [ ] Petitioner proof of citizenship (U.S. passport or U.S. birth certificate).
- [ ] Official marriage certificate.
- [ ] Divorce/death certificates for prior marriages (if applicable).
- [ ] 2x2 passport photos (enough sets for I-485, I-765, and I-131).
- [ ] Bona fide marriage evidence: joint account, lease/mortgage, household bills, insurance, photos, and correspondence.
- [ ] Standardize names across documents and forms.
- [ ] Translate any foreign-language document with certification.
- [ ] Keep copies for filing and originals for interview.

### Step 2: I-693 medical exam

- [ ] Find a USCIS-designated civil surgeon.
- [ ] Schedule the appointment early.
- [ ] Bring passport and vaccine history.
- [ ] Complete required exams and vaccines.
- [ ] Receive sealed I-693 envelope and do not open it.
- [ ] Keep clinic receipts and records.

### Step 3: Form I-130 (petitioner)

- [ ] Download I-130 and instructions (current edition).
- [ ] Complete petitioner and beneficiary details.
- [ ] Provide marriage details and marital history accurately.
- [ ] Sign and date.
- [ ] Attach key evidence: petitioner citizenship proof, marriage certificate, photos, and initial bona fide evidence.
- [ ] Check consistency with I-130A and I-485.

### Step 4: Form I-130A (beneficiary)

- [ ] Complete full biographic history (addresses and employment).
- [ ] Include parents’ information.
- [ ] Sign and date.
- [ ] Attach passport, I-94, and translated birth certificate.
- [ ] Ensure date continuity (no gaps).

### Step 5: Form I-485 (beneficiary)

- [ ] Download I-485 and instructions (current edition).
- [ ] Fill personal data, eligibility basis, and lawful entry.
- [ ] Disclose status history transparently.
- [ ] Answer all eligibility questions accurately.
- [ ] Sign and date.
- [ ] Assemble required attachments: fee, photos, passport, I-94, translated birth certificate, marriage certificate, sealed I-693, petitioner I-864, and joint sponsor I-864 (if any).
- [ ] Final integrity review of the packet.

### Step 6: I-864 (petitioner and joint sponsor)

- [ ] Download I-864 (and I-864A if needed) current edition.
- [ ] Petitioner I-864: household size, current income, taxes, and signature.
- [ ] Joint sponsor I-864: correct household size, qualifying income, proof of status, and signature.
- [ ] Attach IRS transcripts, W-2/1099, employment letters, and pay stubs.
- [ ] Use I-864A when household member income is needed.
- [ ] Confirm required income for the household is met.

### Step 7: I-765 (EAD)

- [ ] Use correct eligibility category for AOS.
- [ ] Sign and date.
- [ ] Attach photos, passport, I-94, and applicable payment.
- [ ] Review SSN data (request issuance if needed).

### Step 8: I-131 (Advance Parole)

- [ ] Select the correct request type for travel during AOS.
- [ ] Describe the purpose of temporary travel.
- [ ] Sign and date.
- [ ] Attach photos, passport, I-94, and applicable fee.
- [ ] Do not travel without approved AP.

### Step 9: Final assembly and mailing

- [ ] Structure the packet with a cover letter and dividers by form.
- [ ] Place G-1145 on top for electronic notifications.
- [ ] Confirm payments and signatures.
- [ ] Scan the complete packet for internal records.
- [ ] Mail to the correct lockbox with tracking.
- [ ] Save proof of mailing and delivery.

### Step 10: Post-filing and follow-up

- [ ] Receive I-797C receipts and verify data.
- [ ] Add cases in myUSCIS.
- [ ] Attend ASC biometrics.
- [ ] Track I-765/I-131 and receipt of EAD/AP.
- [ ] Respond to RFEs on time.
- [ ] Prepare for interview with originals and updated evidence.
- [ ] Track approval, production, and green card delivery.

## RISK MITIGATION

### RFEs and common failures

- [ ] Missing civil document: use the official checklist before mailing.
- [ ] Incomplete I-864: attach taxes and current income proof for all needed sponsors.
- [ ] Missing/expired I-693: verify validity window and sealed envelope.
- [ ] Incomplete translations: use certified translation with translator declaration.
- [ ] Weak bona fide evidence: maintain continuous, organized proof.

### Delays and operations

- [ ] Monitor official processing times.
- [ ] File an inquiry when outside normal time.
- [ ] Update address immediately after moving.
- [ ] Keep a full copy of everything sent.

### Interview

- [ ] Review the submitted form start to finish.
- [ ] Bring originals and organized copies.
- [ ] Bring recent relationship evidence.
- [ ] Answer clearly, consistently, and transparently.

### Travel and status

- [ ] Do not travel without approved AP when applicable.
- [ ] Do not assume AP guarantees admission; prepare return documentation.
- [ ] Avoid long absences from the U.S. during the process and after residency.
