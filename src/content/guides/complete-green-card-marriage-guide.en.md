# Complete Green Card Through Marriage Guide (Educational)

> This guide is educational and generic. USCIS forms, fees, editions, and policies can change. Always verify with official USCIS sources before filing.

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Case Strategy Overview

This scenario presents a highly viable path to permanent residence even with overstay history, when the applicant is the spouse of a U.S. citizen and is eligible as an immediate relative. Immediate relative classification can provide overstay forgiveness for adjustment of status purposes.

Key success factors include immediate relative eligibility, concurrent filing strategy, and complete sponsorship documentation. A critical requirement in many overstay cases is to remain in the U.S. during adjustment, because departure after significant unlawful presence can trigger long inadmissibility bars and waiver complexity.

Recommended approach in similar cases is usually adjustment of status with qualified sponsorship rather than consular processing when departure risk is high.

## 2. TIMELINE MASTER

### Complete Schedule with Critical Dates

### Phase 1: Preparation and Filing (Months 1-2)

- Month 1, Week 1-2: Gather foreign civil documents, arrange certified translations
- Month 1, Week 3: Complete medical examination with USCIS-designated civil surgeon
- Month 1, Week 4: Prepare sponsorship documentation, including joint sponsor if needed
- Month 2, Week 1: File complete package (I-130, I-485, I-864, I-693, I-765, I-131 as applicable)
- Month 2, Week 2-4: Receive receipt notices and establish case tracking

### Phase 2: Initial Processing (Months 2-4)

- Month 2-3: Biometrics appointment scheduling and completion
- Month 3-4: Background checks, work permit processing begins
- Around Month 4: Employment authorization document (EAD) may be approved

### Phase 3: Case Development (Months 4-8)

- Months 4-6: USCIS review and document verification
- Months 6-8: Interview scheduling activity at local field office
- Advance parole approval timing varies widely by office and workload

### Phase 4: Interview and Decision (Months 8-15)

- Months 8-12: Marriage interview at local USCIS office
- Months 10-15: Final decision and card production
- 2-4 weeks: Typical card delivery window after approval in many cases

Estimated timeline is often 10-15 months but varies significantly by location and workload.

### Critical Dependencies

- Medical exam filing requirements follow current USCIS policy
- Sponsorship evidence must be current and complete at filing
- Both spouses should be prepared to attend interview together
- Do not travel without approved advance parole when required

### Supporting Documentation Matrix

For I-130:

- U.S. citizenship proof of petitioner
- Marriage certificate
- Bona fide marriage evidence (joint finances, lease, photos, affidavits)
- Prior divorce decrees when applicable

For I-485:

- Birth certificate with certified English translation
- Passport and lawful entry evidence
- Passport-style photos
- I-693 sealed medical packet
- Arrest/court records if applicable

For I-864:

- Most recent tax returns and IRS transcripts
- Recent pay stubs
- Employment verification letter
- Asset and bank evidence if needed
- Proof of sponsor status in the U.S.

### Translation Requirements

Under federal regulation (8 CFR 103.2(b)(3)), foreign-language documents require full certified English translation with translator certification of competency and accuracy.

Sample certification:

```text
I, [name], certify that I am fluent in English and [source language], and that this is a complete and accurate translation of the document entitled [document name].
Signature: _______________
Date: ___________
[Typed Name and Address]
```

## 4. FINANCIAL BREAKDOWN

### Cost Structure

Typical family-based adjustment costs can include:

- I-130 filing fee
- I-485 filing fee
- Any additional statutory fees in effect
- Medical exam costs (civil surgeon set)
- Optional I-765 and I-131 filing fees
- Translation and document procurement costs

Always confirm current values in official USCIS fee schedules.

### Sponsorship Requirements

Use current Federal Poverty Guidelines and I-864 instructions to calculate household size and required income. If primary sponsor income is insufficient, a qualifying joint sponsor may be required.

### Payments and Methods

- Online payment options for eligible filings
- Check/money order card form options where accepted
- Separate payments may be required per form/package

## 5. OVERSTAY CONSIDERATIONS

### Immediate Relative Protection

Immediate relatives of U.S. citizens may adjust status despite overstay in many scenarios, assuming lawful admission or parole and no disqualifying grounds.

### Unlawful Presence Bars

3-year and 10-year bars are generally triggered by departure after accumulating unlawful presence. In overstay-sensitive cases, departure planning requires legal analysis.

### Travel During Process

Do not depart the U.S. without proper travel authorization where required. Unauthorized travel can lead to adjustment abandonment and inadmissibility issues.

## 6. SPONSORSHIP STRATEGY

### Joint Sponsor Overview

If primary sponsor income is below required threshold, a joint sponsor can independently qualify under I-864 rules.

Typical joint sponsor requirements:

- U.S. citizen, national, or permanent resident
- At least 18 years old
- Domiciled in the U.S.
- Independently meets income requirement for household size

### Documentation for Sponsors

- Separate I-864 forms as required
- Tax returns/transcripts, pay stubs, employment letters
- Assets evidence if income is not sufficient

## 9. RISK MITIGATION

### Common Risks and Mitigations

1. Income insufficiency
- Mitigation: qualified joint sponsor or asset strategy per I-864 rules

2. Overstay and travel mistakes
- Mitigation: do not leave U.S. without legal and procedural readiness

3. Marriage authenticity scrutiny
- Mitigation: robust, consistent bona fide marriage evidence

4. Medical exam problems
- Mitigation: USCIS-designated civil surgeon, sealed packet handling

5. Translation/document errors
- Mitigation: complete certified translations and QC before filing

6. Delays and RFEs
- Mitigation: monitor case, respond quickly, keep records organized

### Emergency Planning

- Urgent travel requests require legal review and proper filings
- Denials require immediate review of deadlines for motion/appeal options
- Material sponsor income changes should be documented and handled promptly
