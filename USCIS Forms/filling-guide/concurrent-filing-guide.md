# Concurrent USCIS Filing Guide

## Key Notes for Concurrent Filing

- **Packet Structure:** While they can be in one envelope, the forms should be clearly separated (e.g., paper-clipped into individual bundles).
- **I-130 Online:** If you file the I-130 online, you must include a copy of the I-130 receipt notice (I-797) in the physical packet with the other forms.
- **Fees:** As of April 1, 2024, separate filing fees are generally required for the I-485, I-765, and I-131.
- **Address:** Mail all forms together to the appropriate USCIS Lockbox based on your location and the specific category (e.g., Chicago, Dallas, Elgin, or Phoenix).

---

When concurrently filing multiple USCIS forms (such as I-130, I-485, I-864, I-765, I-131, etc.), the best practice is to organize the package to facilitate easy processing, using paperclips or binder clips rather than staples, and placing the entire submission in one large envelope.

---

## 1. Overall Package Structure (Top to Bottom)

1. **Cover Letter** — A master cover letter listing all forms being submitted, the names of the applicant and petitioner, and a brief description of the case.
2. **Payment** — Personal checks, money orders (preferable for each form), or Form G-1450 (Credit Card Authorization) on top.
3. **Form G-1145** — E-Notification of Application/Petition Acceptance.
4. **Form G-28** — Notice of Entry of Appearance as Attorney (if using a lawyer).
5. **Individual Application Packages** — Each form should be in its own, self-contained, clipped stack, arranged in the order shown below.

---

## 2. Order of Individual Form Stacks

Stack the forms in this general order, as it reflects the logical flow of approval (dependency):

1. **I-130 / I-130A** (Petition for Alien Relative) — Includes evidence of marriage, birth certificates, etc.
2. **I-864** (Affidavit of Support) — Accompanied by tax transcripts and pay stubs.
3. **I-485** (Adjustment of Status) — Accompanied by birth certificate, passport pages, and photos.
4. **I-693** (Medical Exam) — Sealed envelope, or clipped to the top of I-485 if not sealed.
5. **I-765** (Employment Authorization) — With photos and copy of I-94.
6. **I-131** (Travel Document / Advance Parole) — With photos and copy of I-94.

---

## 3. Key Assembly Tips

- **Do Not Use Heavy Staples** — Use sturdy paperclips or binder clips for each individual form stack.
- **Use Ziploc Bags** — Put passport photos in a Ziploc bag and clip that bag to the corresponding application (usually I-485, I-765, or I-131).
- **Single-Sided Only** — All forms must be printed single-sided.
- **Mark the Envelope** — Clearly label the outside of the envelope with the contents (e.g., "Family-Based Adjustment of Status Package").
- **Copy Everything** — Make a complete copy of the entire package before mailing for your own records.
